📖 Core Grammar Notes

Simple explanation first, then stronger B1/B2 accuracy.

Present Perfect

have / has + V3

Use: experience, recent news, unfinished time, result now.

I have uploaded three videos this week.

I have uploaded yesterday. ✗

Past Perfect

had + V3

Use: an action before another past action.

I had uploaded it before I noticed the mistake.

I had uploaded it yesterday. ✗ if no later past action/context

Future Perfect

will have + V3

Use: completed before a future deadline.

By midnight, I will have gained 50 followers.

Signal Words

TenseCommon signalsImportant warning
Present Perfectever, never, just, already, yet, so far, this weekDo not use with clear finished time: yesterday, last night, in 2020.
Past Perfectbefore, after, by the time, already, just, neverUse only when you connect two past moments.
Future Perfectby tomorrow, by Friday, by the end of, before next weekThe deadline is future; the action is complete before it.

🧭 Timeline Training

Students often fail perfect tenses because they cannot see the time relationship.

Present Perfect Timeline

Past → Now

Experience: I have tried that trend.

Result now: I have lost my password, so I can’t log in.

Past Perfect Timeline

Earlier past → Later past

Earlier: I had chosen the wrong sound.

Later: I realised the mistake.

Future Perfect Timeline

Now → completed before future deadline

By tomorrow, I will have edited the video.

Mini Rule

Ask: Is there one time or two times? If there are two past times, Past Perfect often shows the first one.

🔎 Grammar Accuracy Notes

Common ESL mistakes and band-upgraded language.

Past Simple vs Present Perfect

Finished time: I posted it yesterday.

Unfinished / life: I have posted five videos this week.

Yesterday, last week, two days ago → usually Past Simple.

For / Since

for + duration: for two years

since + starting point: since 2024

He has been a creator for two years.

After / Before

After I had filmed the video, I uploaded it.

Before I uploaded it, I had checked the caption.

In everyday speech, Past Simple is sometimes used after after/before when the order is obvious, but Past Perfect is clearer for exams.

Future Perfect

By the end of the lesson, you will have practised three perfect tenses.

Negative: She won’t have finished by 8 p.m.

Question: Will you have finished by tomorrow?
